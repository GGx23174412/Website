Material Compounds

There are different types of latex depending on their source and production methods. The main types are:

Natural Latex

This is the latex that is harvested from rubber trees, which are organically grown and free of chemicals. Natural latex is processed to create foam layers that are used for various products, such as mattresses, pillows, gloves, etc. Natural latex is durable, elastic, and biodegradable.

link
Organic Latex

This is a form of natural latex that is certified to be organic by a third-party organization. Organic latex is farmed without using pesticides, synthetic fertilizers, or genetically modified organisms. Organic latex also does not contain any fillers or additives that may compromise its quality or safety.

link
Synthetic Latex

This is the latex that is artificially made from petroleum-based materials. It is cheaper and easier to produce than natural latex, but it is also less resilient, less breathable, and more prone to allergies and odors. Synthetic latex is often used for industrial purposes, such as paints, adhesives, and coatings.

link
Blended Latex

This is the latex that is a mixture of natural and synthetic latex. Blended latex usually contains around 15% to 25% natural latex, with the rest being synthetic latex. Blended latex is more affordable than natural latex, but it also loses some of the benefits of natural latex, such as elasticity and healthiness. Blended latex is commonly used for products that require a balance of cost and performance, such as mattresses, furniture, and clothing.
